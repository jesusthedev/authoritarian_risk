Short version

The 2025 Trump administration is ticking a number of classic fascism boxes — centralizing power, politicizing the civil service, criminalizing dissent-adjacent activity, militarizing domestic space, scapegoating out-groups — while wrapping it all in “law and order” branding and patriotic merch.

Assessment framework

Mapping actions to markers: cult of the leader, ultranationalism, suppression of opposition, capture of state machinery, propaganda and enemy construction, and routinized political violence or its credible threat.

What’s actually happened in 2025 (facts + citations)

Capture of the state apparatus

Reinstating and expanding “Schedule F” to purge/reclassify career officials in “policy-influencing” roles. Hiring realignment memos reinforce top‑down control.

Militarization and domestic force

National Guard/active-duty deployments into U.S. cities in response to protests around immigration enforcement; legality contested; rhetoric about “full force.”

Criminalizing dissent and redefining “terrorism”

Orders/memos to counter “domestic terrorism” and “organized political violence,” functionally aimed at protest networks and donors. Congressional allies push labeling resolutions.

Retaliation and justice-system weaponization

Indictment of former FBI Director James Comey alongside public calls for more charges — a break from norms of DOJ independence and a clear retribution signal.

Scapegoating and mass removal machinery

Mass‑deportation posture with detention buildout and revivals of family detention; expanded budgets and operations under ICE.

Propaganda and the “enemy” frame

Continuing delegitimization of the press; “enemy of the people” narratives; policy blueprints echo hostility to the independent media.

Scorecard vs classic fascism markers

  • Leader cult & infallibility framing: loyalty tests and retribution posture.
  • Ultranationalism + law‑and‑order theater: domestic opponents framed as security threats.
  • Suppression of opposition: terror‑labeling of protest currents; legal intimidation of critics.
  • State capture/patronage: Schedule F reboot to purge neutral civil service.
  • Propaganda and enemy construction: media delegitimization; “domestic terrorists” as out‑group.
  • Organized violence or threat thereof: troop deployments and “full force” authorizations.

What’s not proven (yet)

  • Total one‑party rule or canceled elections: not in evidence.
  • Abolition of all checks: courts and states are pushing back; litigation and state resistance remain active friction.

Forward risk outlook (near‑term)

  • Bureaucratic neutralization via expanded Schedule F.
  • Normalization of domestic military use against demonstrations.
  • Rights erosion under deportation surge.

Bottom line

If you strip the branding and listen for the signal, the 2025 trajectory aligns with a modern authoritarian pattern: purge the bureaucracy, criminalize dissent under elastic “terrorism,” use or threaten force domestically, expand removal machinery against a vilified out‑group, and discredit independent media and prosecutors while rewarding loyalists.
